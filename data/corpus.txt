# --- CYP3A4 INTERACTIONS ---
Ketoconazole inhibits CYP3A4 enzyme.
Simvastatin is metabolized by CYP3A4.
Combining CYP3A4 inhibitors with simvastatin increases risk of toxicity.
High levels of simvastatin can cause rhabdomyolysis.

Clarithromycin is a strong CYP3A4 inhibitor.
Atorvastatin is metabolized by CYP3A4.
Combining clarithromycin with atorvastatin increases risk of muscle toxicity.

# --- ANTICOAGULANT INTERACTIONS ---
Warfarin is an anticoagulant that inhibits vitamin K-dependent clotting factors.
Aspirin inhibits platelet aggregation.
Combining warfarin and aspirin increases risk of bleeding.
Concurrent use may increase INR and bleeding complications.

Warfarin is metabolized by CYP2C9 enzyme.
Drugs that inhibit CYP2C9 can increase warfarin levels.

# --- NSAID INTERACTIONS ---
Ibuprofen is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID).
NSAIDs can increase risk of gastrointestinal bleeding.
Combining NSAIDs with anticoagulants increases bleeding risk.

# --- ACE INHIBITOR INTERACTIONS ---
Lisinopril is an ACE inhibitor.
ACE inhibitors can increase potassium levels.
Combining ACE inhibitors with potassium supplements may cause hyperkalemia.

# --- ANTIBIOTIC INTERACTIONS ---
Metronidazole inhibits CYP2C9.
Metronidazole increases warfarin levels.
Combining metronidazole with warfarin increases bleeding risk.

# --- DIABETES DRUG INTERACTIONS ---
Metformin is used to treat type 2 diabetes.
Metformin is cleared renally.
Combining metformin with contrast agents may increase risk of lactic acidosis.
